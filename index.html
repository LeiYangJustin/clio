Problem Statement and Our Solution

Problem Statement While audio guides provide extensive information, it is often hard for visitors to establish connection between specific exhibit details and the verbal descriptions. In contrast, human docents establish eye contact and point directly to specific regions while providing contextual explanations.

We present a robotic tour guide, CLIO, named after the muse of history in Greek mythology. It carries a display as its head and a laser pointer to orient visitors' attention by its head movement and laser pointer. Animated eyes are displayed to build eye contact. An optional low-code interface based on a Large Language Model is offered to coordinate the designed actions with a given narrative script for exhibition.

Hypotheses to Validate the Designed Co-speech Actions

H1: The designed co-speech actions can guide visitors' visual attention in a real-world exhibition set-up.
H2: Tours with designed actions can enhance the visitor's engagement during the tour.
H3: The (arm-less) robotic system with co-speech actions is perceived as natural and intuitive in a tour guide context.

Experiment Setup

Experiment Setup. Given two conditions, Audio-only baseline and CLIO with co-speech actions, we conducted a user study in a mock-up exhibition of historical photographs. Each participant experienced both conditions in a within-subject design. To avoid familiarity effects, we designed two different routes with different sets of photographs for the two conditions. (A) shows the two slightly different routes. (B) shows a participant experiencing the tour with CLIO. We collected eye gaze data via a mobile eye tracker, shown in (C), during the tour to provide quantitative measurements in addition to the qualitative feedback.

Quantitative Analysis through Eye Tracking Data

We compared eye tracking data between the Audio-only baseline and CLIO with co-speech actions. This comparison exemplifies that the participant's gaze at the CLIO condition better aligned with the mentioned exhibits than that at the Audio-only condition.

Qualitative Results

Analysis of the Questionnaire Feedback. (a) is designed to validate H1 regarding the effectiveness of visual attention guidance. A self-developed questionnaire is used. (b) and (c) are adopted from XXX to measure Focused Attention and Reward Factor, demonstrating the engagement level. Subfigures (d-f) are adopted from XXX to measure Animacy, Likeability, and Perceived Intelligence. 5-point Likert scales are used in all questionnaires. In all scales, we observed statistically significant improvements (p<0.001) in the CLIO condition compared to the Audio-only baseline, supporting our hypotheses.
